THE 1975

11.25.14

my first live encounter with the 1975. after years of dreaming i finally got to see my favorite band
perform live. i bought the tickets when they were first announced. after months of begging my parents
to let their 12 year old daughter see a group of middle aged men play songs about drugs, sex, and sadness.
they finally caved. i was the happiest i ever was. i'd only been to one concert before that. it was my first concert
entering my teenage years. i remember that week i was recovering from strep throat so physically, i felt terrible.
i still needed to go. i pretended to be feeling better so everything would still be worth it the opening
acts were the young rising sons & CRUISR. i didn't know anyone who actually listened to these
bands so at the time i asked every person i knew if they were interested. eventually an old friend decided to come with me.
i was coughing and the screaming didn't help. i was so happy the second they came out on stage i started
sobbing on the spot. as a moody preteen that was one of the best nights of my life. i had gotten a taste of the music scene
and there was no stopping how much money i would end up spending in the future. the feeling of the loud music pounding in your
your heart was my favorite. after the show ended i stood outside the venue and threw up on a sidewalk. this was
the begining of a new lifestyle for me.

5.05.16

this was my second opportunity to see my favorite band. i never intended to miss a chance. by this time
it had become normal for me to go to shows. my parents accepted the fact that it would be difficult to keep
me from going out to concerts. the opening acts were the japanese house & wolf alice. both acts were amazing.
i instantly became a fan. the 1975 had just transitioned their whole aesthetic. everything was brighter and more colorful. it was
beautiful seeing all of the lights and hearing the fans shout to the new songs with more emotion. i was a bit farther than i
usually was since the venue didn't offer cheap general admission tickets. i was stuck on the sidelines and the band
still managed to make me feel included. it was so much fun. i was so overwhelmed i cried during most of the songs.
my friend at the time didn't mind. at the time this was my favorite show of all time.

4.17.17

my third time seeing the 1975! i still didn't get general admission because unfortunately it was out
of my budget. instead i was forced into getting balcony seats. what i didn't know before i arrived was how
small and intimate the venue would be. this was the closest i ever was to the 1975. there was one point i made
eyecontact with matty the vocalist. i was so surprised and excited that i jumped and fell and he laughed at me.
it sounds mean but honestly it was one of my favorite experiences. i sang every song and had the time of my life. i got
to meet both opening acts which were colouring & pale waves. they were so nice and down to earth. i am
greatful to have been able to experience that.
